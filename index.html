People & Culture: Leadership Note

As we close another impactful month, we reflect not only on what we have achieved, but on how we continue to evolve as a people-first organization. At EC-Council, our focus remains anchored in building a resilient, future-ready workplace - one that prioritizes well-being, fosters continuous capability building, and champions an inclusive, collaborative culture. We believe organizational success is a direct outcome of empowered individuals who feel valued, supported, and inspired to contribute meaningfully.

Over the past month, we have accelerated several people-centric initiatives aimed at enhancing employee experience, strengthening professional development pathways, and embedding well-being into our everyday ways of working, reflecting our long-term vision of a workplace that is not only high-performing but deeply human-centric.

This newsletter keeps you informed, connected, and engaged with the initiatives, insights, and moments shaping our organizational journey. As we move ahead, we remain committed to listening, learning, and evolving together.

Health & Well-being Intelligence Hub
Did You Know?
🏃
Physical Well-being
Short bouts of movement can be as effective as longer workouts.

Research shows that even "exercise snacks" like 1–2 minutes of brisk stair climbing done a few times a day—can significantly improve cardiovascular fitness and reduce long-term health risks, especially for people with sedentary jobs.

❤️
Emotional Well-being
Naming your emotions can reduce their intensity.

A neuroscience-backed concept called "affect labeling" shows that simply putting feelings into words (e.g., “I feel overwhelmed”) can lower activity in the brain’s stress centers, helping you regulate emotions more effectively.

🧠
Cognitive Well-being
Your brain treats unfinished tasks as "open loops".

A psychological phenomenon known as the "Zeigarnik Effect" shows that incomplete tasks tend to stay active in your mind, causing mental clutter and stress - while writing them down or planning next steps can help your brain “release” them.
